Keren as a Lone Soldier

Even as a Lone Soldier, she entered the officer training program, and with a very low level of Hebrew, but she mastered Hebrew extremely rapidly, finishing first in her class and first in the training academy.

61. I love seeing old Israeli men sitting at a table, laughing, playing shesh-besh, eating nuts and smoking- knowing that they have probably been friends for years and been through a lot together.
62. I love seeing a group of guys my age, that are so close and are like brothers.. knowing that in 40 years time they will be what I described above.
64. (Only those who have experienced this feeling will be able to understand it) I love that feeling you get when you just realize. You just have a moment of realization of how lucky it is to be here. Be it on a memorial day, or at the top of a mountain during a hike with friends, or on a packed bus. There are those moments where you all of a sudden appreciate where you are. In the state of Israel. The Jewish homeland. Those moments, are truly great.
17. I love that last year during the Memorial ceremony for the fallen soldiers of Israel, I met a Holocaust survivor who spent the evening telling me his story and held my hand during the Hatikva.
Keren Hajioff, Article in The Israel Forever Foundation

Keren as a Newly Minted Officer in the IDF


Spokesperson for the IDF Northern Command - Social Media Innovation

As Head of Social Media for the IDF, Keren innovated a completely novel approach for IDF social media tweets - edgy, snarky, funny, tweets that vastly improved the public perception of the IDF, especially the GenZ generation.

'We can add the IDF Twitter account’s snark to the inventory of Israel’s lethal arsenal.''

—i24 news anchor Eylon Levy Tweet

During these years, Completed a bachelor’s degree in Political Science and a master's degree in Political Communication, both from Bar Ilan University in Israel.

Years as Senior Advisor to Two Prime Ministers of Israel: Iran strategy, Abraham Accords, Ukraine/Russia Mediation
